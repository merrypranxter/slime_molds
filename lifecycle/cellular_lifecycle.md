# Cellular (Dictyostelid) Lifecycle

Dictyostelids are **social amoebae** — individually ordinary single cells that, under starvation, collectively decide to become a multicellular organism. The lifecycle is conceptually among the strangest in biology: solitary hunters merging into a slug, then building a monument where some cells sacrifice themselves so others can reproduce.

## **Stage 1: Spore**

**Size:** 5–10 μm (microscopic)

**Visual traits:**
- Ovoid to round
- Smooth walls, compact
- Colors: pale cream, translucent white, light tan
- Packed in sorus mass at tip of sorocarp

**Art potential:**
- Minimalist dot clusters
- Pale ghost particles
- Dense packed geometry

**Duration:** Dormant for weeks to years in unfavorable conditions

---

## **Stage 2: Amoeba** (Vegetative Phase)

**Size:** 10–20 μm (microscopic)

**Visual traits:**
- Single-celled blob, pseudopod-extending
- Translucent, colorless to pale cream
- Hunts bacteria by engulfment
- Completely independent, no communication with neighbors

**Art potential:**
- Solitary hunters in a bacterial landscape
- Pseudopod extension as grasping geometry
- Individual agency before the collective

**Duration:** Days to weeks (as long as food is available)

---

## **Stage 3: cAMP Signal Waves** ⭐ **ICONIC STAGE**

**Size:** Population-level (cm scale spiral patterns)

**Visual traits:**
- Cells begin releasing cyclic AMP (cAMP) in pulses
- Neighboring cells relay the signal outward
- Creates **spiral and concentric ring patterns** visible under microscope/timelapse
- Like waves on a pond, but made of chemical pulses in a lawn of amoebae
- Colors: dark cells on pale substrate, moving patterns

**Art potential:**
- **SIGNAL SPIRAL:** Concentric ring chemotaxis waves, reaction-diffusion logic
- **CHEMICAL DEMOCRACY:** Collective vote without a brain
- **PULSE PROPAGATION:** Oscillating wave interference patterns
- **BELOUSOV-ZHABOTINSKY analog:** Chemical clock aesthetics

**Duration:** Hours (rapid transition to aggregation)

---

## **Stage 4: Aggregation Streams** ⭐ **ICONIC STAGE**

**Size:** mm-scale streams converging to cm-scale center

**Visual traits:**
- Cells stream toward aggregation center in rivers
- Rivers branch like delta in reverse — tributaries joining
- Thick cell highways, dark against substrate
- Multiple streams merging creates branching river-delta geometry (reversed)
- Colors: dense cream/tan streams on pale agar or soil

**Art potential:**
- **REVERSE RIVER DELTA:** Tributaries converging to one source
- **COLLECTIVE MIGRATION:** Individual wills surrendering to group
- **TOPOLOGICAL CONVERGENCE:** Branching geometry folding inward
- **CELLULAR HIGHWAY:** Biochemical traffic flow

**Duration:** Hours to a day

---

## **Stage 5: Slug / Grex** ⭐ **ICONIC STAGE**

**Size:** 0.5–2 mm (visible to naked eye)

**Visual traits:**
- Elongated, slug-like body
- Translucent to pale cream/amber
- Moves as single unit toward light and heat gradients
- Tip cells (prestalk zone) lead; rear cells (prespore zone) follow
- Leaves mucus trail on substrate
- Visible internal cell movement when backlit

**Art potential:**
- **ALIEN ORGANISM:** Collective animal — thousands of cells acting as one body
- **SOCIAL CONTRACT MADE VISIBLE:** Front cells already destined to die for back cells
- **TRANSLUCENT MIGRATION:** Backlit slug as ghost creature
- **SACRIFICE ARCHITECTURE:** Predetermined cell fate visible in body gradient

**Duration:** Hours to days (migrates toward optimal fruiting location)

---

## **Stage 6: Culmination**

**Size:** ~0.5–1 mm (very compact)

**Visual traits:**
- Slug tips upright (vertical orientation)
- Dramatic transformation begins: front cells become stalk, rear cells become spores
- Stalk cells pour vacuolate fluid into cellulose tubes — self-sacrifice made structural
- Rising tip carries spore mass upward
- Visible as upward-pointing tiny pale structure

**Art potential:**
- **SACRIFICE ARCHITECTURE:** Cells dying to hold up future generation
- **INVERSION MOMENT:** The tip becomes the bottom; everything flips
- **TRANSFORMATION SEQUENCE:** Timelapse of the slug standing up

**Duration:** Hours

---

## **Stage 7: Sorocarp** ⭐ **ICONIC STAGE**

**Size:** 1–3 mm (visible to naked eye, requires magnification for detail)

**Visual traits:**
- Thin transparent cellulose stalk (sorus stalk / sorophore)
- Spherical to ovoid sorus at tip containing spore mass
- Colors: translucent white stalk, pale cream/tan sorus
- Often branched (some species)
- Minimalist — spare, elegant, lollipop or candelabra form
- Can be massed together as fruiting arrays

**Art potential:**
- **MINIMALIST MONUMENT:** The simplest possible architecture
- **LOLLIPOP GEOMETRY:** Sphere-on-stick at microscale
- **CANDELABRA ARRAY:** Multiple branching sorocarps
- **CELLULAR MEMORIAL:** Stalk cells are dead; sorus cells are future

**Duration:** Days to weeks (drying, spore dispersal)

---

## **Stage 8: Spore Release**

**Visual traits:**
- Sorus dries and releases spores as powdery mass
- Spores disperse by wind, rain, passing animals
- Stalk remains as dried cellulose ghost structure

**Art potential:**
- Ghost stalk remains
- Spore puff minimalism
- Translucent skeleton after release

## **Cellular vs. Plasmodial Lifecycle Comparison**

| Feature | Plasmodial (Myxomycetes) | Cellular (Dictyostelids) |
|---------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| Cells fuse? | Yes — become one giant cell | No — stay separate, cooperate |
| Plasmodium? | Yes (multinucleate single cell) | No (aggregate of separate cells) |
| Vein networks? | Yes (cytoplasmic streaming) | No |
| Slug stage? | No | Yes (multicellular migratory body) |
| Sacrifice behavior? | No | Yes (stalk cells die for spores) |
| Art identity | Network + foam + gothic towers | Signal waves + social collapse + minimalist monument |
| Scale | cm to m (plasmodium) | mm (sorocarp) |
| Color | Often vivid (yellow, red, orange) | Usually pale/translucent |
| Drama type | Expansion + architecture | Aggregation + sacrifice |

## **Notes**

- Dictyostelids are NOT slime molds in the traditional sense — they're social amoebae, more closely related to animals than to myxomycetes
- The sacrifice behavior (stalk cells die so spore cells can reproduce) is one of the most studied examples of altruism in biology
- cAMP wave patterns are a model system for reaction-diffusion mathematics and morphogenesis
- *Dictyostelium discoideum* is a major model organism — heavily researched, well-documented visually
