# Myxomycetes Orders - Visual Organization

Myxomycetes (plasmodial slime molds) are organized into ~6 major orders based on fruiting body structure and spore characteristics. This is a **visual-first** breakdown, not exhaustive taxonomy.

---

## **Order: Physarales**

**Visual identity:** Massive cushion-like aethalia, lime-encrusted peridia, often bright yellow/white, foamy or crusty

**Key genera:**
- *Fuligo* - Dog vomit slime molds, sulfur yellow foams
- *Physarum* - Most diverse genus, often vein-forming plasmodia
- *Badhamia* - Lime-crusted cushions
- *Diderma* - White chalky frost coatings
- *Craterium* - Goblet-shaped sporangia
- *Didymium* - White-dusted or iridescent sporangia

**Iconic species:**
- *Fuligo septica* - Scrambled egg slime, sulfur foam pillows
- *Physarum polycephalum* - Yellow vein networks, maze-solver
- *Badhamia utricularis* - Clustered lime cushions
- *Diderma hemisphaericum* - White frost hemispheres

**Art hooks:**
- Foamy architecture
- Diseased luxury textiles
- Calcified alien masses
- Pulsing vein networks
- Crusty planetary terrain

---

## **Order: Trichiales**

**Visual identity:** Coral-like tufts, lace, candelabra, often red/orange/yellow, with elastic capillitium that expands dramatically

**Key genera:**
- *Arcyria* - Coral tufts, red/pink/yellow
- *Trichia* - Yellow/orange candelabra clusters
- *Hemitrichia* - Serpentine branching, yellow/red
- *Metatrichia* - Reddish tufted networks

**Iconic species:**
- *Arcyria denudata* - Red coral tufts
- *Arcyria cinerea* - Grey lace structures
- *Trichia varia* - Yellow candelabra clusters
- *Hemitrichia serpula* - Serpentine yellow networks

**Art hooks:**
- Coral reef analogs
- Lace and filigree
- Candelabra branching
- Tuft explosions
- Elastic fraying structures

---

## **Order: Stemonitidales**

**Visual identity:** Metallic blacks, purples, iridescent surfaces, pin-like or feathery, often on a dark stalk (stipe), gothic aesthetic

**Key genera:**
- *Stemonitis* - Feathery black pins
- *Comatricha* - Gothic black goblets
- *Lamproderma* - Iridescent metallic surfaces
- *Stemonitopsis* - Tall feathery clusters

**Iconic species:**
- *Stemonitis fusca* - Black feathery pins
- *Stemonitis axifera* - Chocolate-brown feather clusters
- *Lamproderma scintillans* - Iridescent violet/blue surfaces
- *Comatricha nigra* - Gothic black goblets

**Art hooks:**
- Metallic gothic structures
- Oil slick iridescence
- Pin forests
- Feathery plumes
- Dark elegance

---

## **Order: Liceales**

**Visual identity:** Often tiny, simple, sometimes net-like or sessile (no stalk), minimal structure, can be widespread films

**Key genera:**
- *Licea* - Tiny sessile sporangia, often reddish-brown
- *Cribraria* - Net-like cages, bead towers
- *Dictydiaethalium* - Net-like aethalia

**Iconic species:**
- *Cribraria argillacea* - Terracotta bead towers with netted cages
- *Cribraria cancellata* - Delicate lace cages
- *Licea biforis* - Tiny sessile reddish blobs

**Art hooks:**
- Bead jewelry analogs
- Lace cages
- Minimal brutalism
- Terracotta ceramics
- Sessile jewels

---

## **Order: Echinosteliales**

**Visual identity:** TINY - often <1mm, stalked, minimalist, requires microscopy for best viewing, protostelid-adjacent

**Key genera:**
- *Echinostelium* - Minute stalked sporangia with spiny spores

**Iconic species:**
- *Echinostelium minutum* - Microscopic stalked jewels

**Art hooks:**
- Micro-ornament logic
- Jewel-scale minimalism
- Spiny surfaces at extreme magnification

---

## **Order: Ceratiomyxales**

**Visual identity:** White/translucent pillars, coral-like branching, external spores (unusual), often aquatic-adjacent

**Key genus:**
- *Ceratiomyxa* - White coral pillars, translucent gel structures

**Iconic species:**
- *Ceratiomyxa fruticulosa* - White coral pillars on rotting wood
- *Ceratiomyxa morchella* - Morel-like white structures

**Art hooks:**
- Frost cathedrals
- Translucent gel architecture
- Coral analogs
- Ice formations
- Aquatic-terrestrial hybrids

---

## Visual Archetype Cross-Reference

**Foams & Crusts:** Physarales (Fuligo, Physarum, Badhamia, Diderma)  
**Coral & Lace:** Trichiales (Arcyria, Trichia, Hemitrichia)  
**Bead Towers & Cages:** Liceales (Cribraria)  
**Metallic Gothic:** Stemonitidales (Stemonitis, Lamproderma, Comatricha)  
**Frost & Translucent:** Ceratiomyxales (Ceratiomyxa)  
**Micro Jewels:** Echinosteliales (Echinostelium)

---

## Notes

This is not a complete taxonomic treatment. There are ~12-15 orders in Myxomycetes depending on classification system, but these 6 cover the majority of visually distinct and art-relevant morphologies.
